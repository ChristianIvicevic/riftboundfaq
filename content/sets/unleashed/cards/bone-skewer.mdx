## Can units with "I can't be chosen by enemy spells and abilities." be chosen with Bone Skewer? [#targeting-protection]

Yes.

Passive abilities of permanents are only active while on the board,<Rule number="365.1" /> so that protection — as seen on units like [Ruin Runner](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--sfd-105-221) and [Baron Nashor](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--unl-147-219) — has no effect while the unit is in the opponent's hand.
Beyond that, Bone Skewer never targets the chosen unit in the first place — cards in hand are never targets because the hand is not a public zone,<Rule number="355.10.a" /> so there is no finalization step at which a unit there could be declared as a target.<Rule number="355.5" />

## Can the opponent trigger an optional additional cost effect when playing a unit via Bone Skewer, even though its costs are ignored? [#optional-additional-costs]

Yes.

The phrase "any and all costs" in Bone Skewer's text is intentionally broader than the standard "ignoring its cost" wording.
Standard cost-ignoring only zeroes out a card's base Energy and Power costs,<Rule number="356.1.b.1" /> but "any and all" extends that to additional costs as well.
Despite this, the opponent may still declare their intention to pay an optional additional cost during step 2 of playing the card — the decision to pay is made before costs are determined.
Once declared, that additional cost is itself subject to "any and all costs" and is therefore already 0 when it comes time to pay.<Rule number="356.4.f" />
An optional additional cost is considered paid based on the decision to pay, not the amount actually spent,<Rule number="356.4.f.1" /> so the condition is fully satisfied.
Any conditional effect tied to "if you paid the additional cost" therefore triggers normally.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You play Bone Skewer and choose [Nami, Headstrong](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--unl-052-219) from your opponent's hand.
	Your opponent is instructed to play Nami to the chosen battlefield, ignoring any and all costs.
	As part of that process, they declare their intention to pay the optional additional cost of <Power type="calm" />.
	That cost is already 0, so nothing is actually paid — but because they chose to pay it, the condition is met.
	Nami's triggered ability fires, stunning an enemy unit.
</Callout>
