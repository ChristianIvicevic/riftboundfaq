## When do triggered abilities trigger? [#triggered-ability-timing]

When their condition is met, triggered abilities go on the chain immediately, regardless of whose turn it is (<Rule number="376.3" />, <Rule number="376.3.a" />).

The <Reaction /> keyword is not required for this to work on an opponent's turn.
Unlike spells and activated abilities which require <Reaction /> to be played outside your turn, triggered abilities are automatic and will trigger whenever their condition is met, on any player's turn.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	[Blade Dancer](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--sfd-195-221) triggers when you choose a friendly unit.
	This can happen on your opponent's turn, for example when you play a <Reaction /> spell that targets one of your own units.
</Callout>

## Can I react to triggered and activated abilities? [#reacting-to-abilities]

Yes, with one important exception: abilities that use the <Add /> keyword.

All triggered and activated abilities are placed on the chain.
However, abilities that add resources resolve immediately when finalized, which means players do not receive priority to react to them (<Rule number="333.1.c" />, <Rule number="416.2.a" />).

## When do I pay costs within instructions using "may"? [#costs-within-instructions]

On resolution.

In rules text phrased as "[do X] to [do Y]," the [do X] part is considered a cost within instructions.
These costs within instructions are not targets for the purposes of finalising an ability and are not paid during finalisation (<Rule number="352.10" />, <Rule number="352.10.c" />, <Rule number="352.10.c.1" />).
Therefore, opponents who react to this triggered ability will not know whether the controller will choose to pay the cost or not.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	[Vayne, Hunter](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--ogn-035-298)'s "When I conquer, you may pay <Energy value={1} /> to return me to my owner's hand." will trigger when you conquer, opening a moment of opportunity to react.
	On resolution of this ability, you can decline to pay the <Energy value={1} /> cost, and the ability will resolve to no effect.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
	This reasoning also applies to spells and activated abilities that use the "[do X] to [do Y]" pattern.
	For example, the spell [Guards!](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--sfd-154-221) reads "Play a 2 Might Sand Soldier unit token.
	You may pay <Power type="order" /> to ready it."
	The cost to ready the token is paid on resolution of the spell.
</Callout>
