## What does "play" mean on a card? [#what-does-play-mean]

It depends on where it appears — "play" has three distinct functional meanings in Riftbound rules text.

In trigger conditions — "When you play," as seen on <Card name="Ravenbloom Student" /> and <Card name="Darius, Trifarian" /> — it means resolve.
These abilities trigger once playing the card is complete, which happens at its resolution.<Rule number="419.4" /><Rule number="419.4.a" />
For units and gear, that's the instant they're finalized, since both resolve immediately without priority being passed;<Rule number="337.2" /> for spells, resolution is a separate, later step, reached only after finalization and any reactions have played out.<Rule number="340.1" />

In conditional statements checking whether a card has been played — "if you've played," as seen on <Card name="Battering Ram" /> and <Card name="Crescent Guardian" /> — it means finalize, not resolve.
A pending item satisfies this the moment it's finalized.<Rule number="329.3" />
The <Legion /> keyword makes this precise: it's written as "if you have played another card this turn,"<Rule number="812.1.b.1" /> but the dependent ability is active as long as that other card "has been Finalized on the same turn" — not resolved.<Rule number="812.1.c" />

As an instruction — "play a card," as seen on <Card name="Here to Help" /> and <Card name="Fizz, Trickster" /> — it means place the card on the chain and queue it for finalization.<Rule number="419.1" />
This is the general, baseline meaning: the limited action of putting a card on the chain, nothing more.
