## Does the target's controller draw if the unit isn't killed? [#draw-if-not-killed]

Yes, as long as the target is still legal when Hidden Blade resolves.

The phrase "its controller" is a referent — information drawn from the target, recognised by the word "its".<Rule number="359.3.f.1" />
Referents are checked at the moment the instruction executes, not when the spell was played.<Rule number="359.3.f.2" />
Because the target is still legal when Hidden Blade resolves, the referent is accessible and the draw executes, regardless of whether the kill succeeded.<Rule number="359.3.e.12.a" />

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You play Hidden Blade targeting an opponent's unit.
	They respond with [Zhonya's Hourglass](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--ogn-077-298), which replaces the unit's death.
	The unit is not killed. Because it was still a legal target when Hidden Blade resolved, the opponent draws 2.
</Callout>

## Does the controller draw if the target becomes illegal? [#draw-if-illegal]

No.
An illegal target is entirely unaffected by the spell, and the draw is no exception.

An illegal target is unaffected by the spell as it resolves.<Rule number="359.3.e.5" />
The phrase "its controller" is a referent — information drawn from the target — and because the target is now illegal, that referent returns null and the draw is ignored.<Rule number="359.3.f.1" /><Rule number="359.3.f.2" /><Rule number="359.3.e.12" />

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You play Hidden Blade targeting an opponent's unit at a battlefield.
	They respond by moving it to their base using [Flash](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--ogs-011-024).
	Hidden Blade resolves. The unit is no longer at a battlefield, so it is an illegal target.
	The unit is not killed and the opponent does not draw.
</Callout>
