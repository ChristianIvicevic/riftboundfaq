## Does Burn or Minefield trigger Nocturne's ability? [#burn-and-minefield]

<Callout type="warn" title="Rules citation needed">
	"Look" is not yet defined as its own numbered action the way "reveal" is.
	The core rules consistently treat it as a parallel, explicitly-instructed action alongside reveal wherever both are mentioned, and this ruling relies on that pattern — an official update giving "look" its own definition would let this be cited directly.
</Callout>

No — <Burn /> only puts cards into your trash; it never looks at or reveals anything, and Nocturne's ability only cares about those two specific actions.
<Card name="Minefield" />'s printed ability works the same way, and this won't change even if its wording is later updated to explicitly read <Burn value={2} />.

Nocturne's ability reads "As you look at or reveal me from the top of your deck, you may banish me. If you do, you may play me for <Universal />."
That phrasing ties the permission to the specific action of looking at or revealing Nocturne — the same construction the rules use elsewhere to key an effect to one exact moment rather than a general state, such as choices made "As I am played."<Rule number="355.1" />

Reveal is a defined action, and a player may only reveal cards when a game effect specifically instructs them to.<Rule number="424.1" /><Rule number="424.2" /><Rule number="424.2.a" />
"Look" doesn't have its own numbered definition yet, but it's treated the same way — the main deck is secret information specifically because it "cannot be looked through or revealed unless instructed by a card effect."<Rule number="103.2.e" /><Rule number="103.2.e.1" />

<Burn /> does neither of these — it puts cards from the top of your main deck directly into your trash, with no instruction to look at or reveal them.<Rule number="431.1.b" />
<Card name="Minefield" />'s printed ability, "When you conquer here, put the top 2 cards of your Main Deck into your trash," does exactly the same thing — it's already functionally identical to <Burn value={2} />, so an errata formalizing it as such wouldn't add a look or reveal instruction that isn't already there.
Either way, those cards only become visible because they land in the trash, a public zone by default,<Rule number="108.2.d" /> not because anyone looked at or revealed them first.

Since neither instructs anyone to look at or reveal Nocturne, its ability is never triggered by <Burn /> or by <Card name="Minefield" />.
