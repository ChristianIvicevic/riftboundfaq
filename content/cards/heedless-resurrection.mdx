## If Heedless Resurrection is countered, is the killed unit returned? [#no-refunds]

No, as the kill is an additional cost and countering a spell never refunds costs already paid.

Killing a friendly unit is an additional cost required to play Heedless Resurrection.
A countered spell does nothing and is not considered to have been played.<Rule number="425.1.a" /><Rule number="425.1.b" />
Countering does not refund any costs paid to play a card, including additional costs.<Rule number="425.1.c" /><Rule number="425.1.c.1" />
That cost is paid before the spell resolves, so the killed unit is not returned.

## Can I kill a unit with Heedless Resurrection's cost and then resurrect that same unit? [#self-resurrection]

No, as the resurrection target must already be in your trash when you choose it, before the kill cost is paid.

When playing Heedless Resurrection, you must choose the unit to resurrect during step 2 of finalization — the "Make relevant choices" step.<Rule number="355.5" />
At that point, the unit you intend to kill is still on the board, not yet in your trash.
"A unit from your trash" must be in your trash to be a valid target,<Rule number="355.9.a" /> so a living unit on the board cannot be chosen.
The additional cost — killing a friendly unit — is paid in step 4,<Rule number="357.2" /> after all choices are already locked in.<Rule number="355.15" />

## If Heedless Resurrection gains Repeat, do I need to kill a friendly unit for each repetition? [#repeat-cost]

No.
The additional cost to kill a friendly unit is paid once, as part of playing the spell.

Additional costs like this are paid during step 4 of playing the card,<Rule number="357.2" /> not during resolution.
<Repeat /> makes the spell's instructions execute an additional time upon resolution,<Rule number="820.1.d.1" /> but does not re-run costs that were already paid while playing — "as you play me" instructions execute once, before the <Repeat /> execution ever occurs.<Rule number="135.2.b.3" />

No matter how many times <Repeat /> is paid, the spell is only played once,<Rule number="820.3.a" /> so you kill only one friendly unit.
