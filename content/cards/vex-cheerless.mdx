## Do opponents have to pay resources to play cards from Hidden while Vex is in combat? [#hidden-cost-increase]

Yes, cards played from Hidden by your opponents still cost <Energy value={1} /><Universal /> when Vex is in combat.

When a card with Hidden is played from its facedown state, it is played "ignoring its base cost".<Rule number="811.1.b" />
During step 3 of playing a card ("Determine Total Cost"), the following happens in order:

1. Set base costs to zero:<Rule number="356.1" /> If an ability or instruction allows you to "ignore" costs, set the base cost(s) to zero. Hidden cards played from facedown have their base cost set to <Energy value={0} />.
2. Apply cost increases:<Rule number="356.3" /> Vex's ability increases enemy spell costs by <Energy value={1} /><Universal />. This is applied to the total cost even though the base cost is zero
3. Apply discounts:<Rule number="356.4" /> No discounts apply in this scenario

The total cost ends up being <Energy value={1} /><Universal /> because cost increases are applied after base costs are set to zero.

## Does Vex reduce Deflect costs when you play spells? [#deflect-cost-reduction]

Yes, the cost reduction applies to <Deflect /> costs when you target an opponent's permanent while she's in combat.

<Deflect /> is a mandatory additional cost.<Rule number="356.2.a.2" /><Rule number="809.1.d" />
When you play a spell or ability that targets an opponent's permanent with it, the <Deflect /> value is added to your total cost during "Apply additional costs" when determining total cost.<Rule number="356.2" />

Vex's <Energy value={1} /><Universal /> discount is applied during "Apply discounts",<Rule number="356.4" /> which comes after additional costs are applied.
Discounts can reduce additional costs, including to 0.<Rule number="356.4.f" />
