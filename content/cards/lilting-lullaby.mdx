## If my opponent targets my spell with Lilting Lullaby and I counter my own spell in response, can I still play spells? [#own-counter]

Yes.
Countering your own spell removes it from the chain, so Lilting Lullaby has no valid target when it resolves — and with nothing to counter, the linked restriction on playing spells is also ignored.

When you counter your own spell, it is cleared from the chain.<Rule number="425.1.a" />
"Spell" in a targeting context refers to objects on the chain.<Rule number="355.9.a.2" />
By the time Lilting Lullaby resolves, its target is no longer on the chain and no longer meets the targeting requirements — this is mistargeting.<Rule number="359.3.e.2" /><Rule number="359.3.e.9" />

Because all of Lilting Lullaby's first instruction's targets are now invalid, the "Counter a spell" instruction does not execute.<Rule number="359.3.e.7" />

"Counter a spell" and "Its controller can't play spells this turn" are linked instructions — the second references the controller of the spell from the first.
Since the earlier linked instruction was ignored, the later one is ignored as well.<Rule number="359.3.e.14.a" />
