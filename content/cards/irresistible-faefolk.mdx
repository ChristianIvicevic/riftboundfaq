## If I move Irresistible Faefolk to an open battlefield and pull an enemy unit there, who is the attacker? [#who-attacks]

You — the Irresistible Faefolk player — are the attacker.

When Irresistible Faefolk moves to an open (uncontrolled) battlefield, that move applies the contested status to the battlefield.<Rule number="445" /><Rule number="187.3.a" />
During the cleanup that follows the move, a combat is staged because units from opposing players are now present at the contested battlefield.<Rule number="323.9" />
When combat opens, the attacker is defined as the player whose unit(s) applied the contested status.<Rule number="459.2.b.1" />
That is you — the player who moved Irresistible Faefolk.
The opponent whose unit was pulled in is the defender.<Rule number="459.2.b.2" />

## If I play Irresistible Faefolk directly to a battlefield, does its ability trigger? [#playing-vs-moving]

No — playing a unit to a battlefield is not a move, so the ability does not trigger.

A move is defined as a permanent changing its position from one location on the board to another.<Rule number="441.1" />
Playing a card involves it entering the board from a non-board zone (such as your hand), which is a zone change, not a move.<Rule number="441.2" />
Because Irresistible Faefolk's ability specifically triggers when it *moves* to a battlefield, playing it directly there does not satisfy that trigger condition.
