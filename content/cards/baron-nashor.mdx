## Does Baron Nashor move to the Baron Pit when it's created as part of playing him? [#baron-move]

No.
Baron Nashor enters the [Baron Pit](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--unl-t01) directly — his entry location is replaced, not his position on the board.
A move is defined as a permanent changing position from one space on the board to another.<Rule number="441.1" />
Baron Nashor is entering the board for the first time (a zone change), which is not a move.<Rule number="441.2" />

## What happens after Baron Nashor enters the Baron Pit? [#baron-pit-showdown]

A non-combat showdown begins.
The [Baron Pit](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--unl-t01) is newly created and has no controller when Baron Nashor enters it.
Being played to a battlefield applies contested status to it, provided the entering unit's controller does not already control that battlefield.<Rule number="187.3.a.1" />
Because the Baron Pit is uncontrolled at that moment, a non-combat showdown is staged during the resulting cleanup — the same mechanism that triggers when [Sneaky Deckhand](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--ogn-176-298) is played to an open battlefield.<Rule number="316.5.b" /><Rule number="323.8" /><Rule number="344.2" />

If an opposing unit arrives at the Baron Pit during the showdown the non-combat showdown becomes a combat showdown.<Rule number="316.5.b.1.a" />

If the showdown closes with only Baron Nashor's controller's units present at the Baron Pit, and they have not yet scored it this turn, they conquer it and score.<Rule number="348.2.a" /><Rule number="348.2.a.1" />

## Does the Baron Pit need to be scored to win by conquering? [#baron-pit-victory]

Yes.
Winning by conquering at match point requires having scored every battlefield on the board that turn — including the Baron Pit.<Rule number="466.1.b.2" />
If it hasn't been scored, the winning conquer draws a card instead.

Winning by hold is unaffected, as it has no such requirement.<Rule number="466.1.b.1" />

## Can I play Baron Nashor while an opponent's Mageseeker Warden is at a battlefield? [#mageseeker-warden]

It depends on whether the [Baron Pit](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--unl-t01) is already on the board.

**If the Baron Pit is not yet on the board**, Baron Nashor enters the Baron Pit, not your base.
As Baron Nashor is played, his "as you play me" instruction fires during the playing process — the Baron Pit battlefield token is added to the board.<Rule number="135.2.b.3" /><Rule number="184.9" />
Under [Mageseeker Warden](https://riftbound.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/card-gallery/#card-gallery--ogn-070-298)'s restriction, your base is the only valid playing location — where Baron Nashor is nominally played to — but his replacement effect overrides where he actually enters.<Rule number="355.2.a" />
His "If you do, I enter there" then functions as a replacement effect (per Riot ruling) that intercedes when he would enter the board, replacing his entry to his base with entry to the Baron Pit.<Rule number="369" /><Rule number="370.1.a" />

**If the Baron Pit is already on the board**, the "as you play me" instruction does nothing — the Baron Pit token is only added if it's not there already — so the "If you do" condition is not met.
Baron Nashor simply enters his base, the only valid playing location under Mageseeker Warden's restriction.<Rule number="355.2.a" />

<Callout type="warn" title="Rules citation needed">
	Baron Nashor's "If you do, I enter there" lacks the "would" or "instead" language the rules use to identify replacement effects,<Rule number="369.1" /> and entry-location-modifying instructions of this form are not explicitly addressed.<Rule number="369.2" />
	Riot has confirmed the intent, but clearer rules language may be needed.
</Callout>
