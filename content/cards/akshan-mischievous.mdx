## Your opponent plays their own Akshan and takes control of the gear your Akshan already controls. What happens when one or both Akshans die? [#dual-akshan-control]

Whichever Akshan was played most recently determines control of the gear for as long as it remains on the board.

Both Akshans generate a continuous effect that changes the gear's controller — an inherent trait of every game object.<Rule number="472.1" /><Rule number="472.1.a" />
Since both effects operate in the same layer and neither depends on the other,<Rule number="473" /> they are ordered by timestamp: the earliest-established effect applies first, and the most recently established effect applies last, winning out.<Rule number="475" /><Rule number="475.3" />
Your opponent's Akshan was played after yours, so it carries the later timestamp — its control effect is the final one applied, and your opponent controls the gear.

When an Akshan leaves the board, its "until I leave the board" duration ends and its control effect ceases immediately.
The remaining effects are then re-evaluated in timestamp order:

- **Your opponent's Akshan dies**: their effect ceases.
Only your Akshan's effect remains, giving you control of the gear.
- **Your Akshan dies**: your effect ceases.
Your opponent's Akshan's effect was already the deciding one — they continue to control the gear unchanged.
- **Both Akshans die**: both effects cease.
Control reverts to your opponent, since it was their gear to begin with.
If the gear is unattached and now finds itself in a base it doesn't belong to, it will be recalled to your opponent's base during the next cleanup.

## Does the enemy Dazzling Aurora I take control of with Akshan also trigger at the end of this turn? [#aurora-timing]

No — you gain control of it too late in the turn for its trigger to fire.

<Card name="Dazzling Aurora" />'s trigger condition is "At the end of your turn."
"At" identifies a triggered ability keyed to a specific point in time during the turn sequence<Rule number="383.1" /> — here, the Ending Step, when end-of-turn game effects take place.<Rule number="317.1" /><Rule number="317.1.a" />
A trigger's condition is evaluated once, immediately after that point in time is reached<Rule number="383.2.c" /> — it is not re-checked continuously for as long as the game remains in the Ending Phase.

Akshan's own play effect only triggers once he is finalized on the board — a separate, later event<Rule number="383.4.a" /><Rule number="383.4.a.2" /> — and that is when you gain control of the enemy's Dazzling Aurora.

By the time that happens, your Ending Step's "at the end of your turn" condition has already been evaluated — once, for whatever you controlled at that instant.
The enemy Dazzling Aurora wasn't yours yet then, so it never gets checked this turn; there's no second check waiting for it later in the phase.
It will trigger normally at the end of your future turns, for as long as you continue to control it.
