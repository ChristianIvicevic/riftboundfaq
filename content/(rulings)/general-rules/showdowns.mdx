## When does a showdown close? [#showdown-close]

Once initiated, a showdown *never* closes abruptly.
A showdown will only end once all players have consecutively passed focus on an empty chain.<Rule number="348" />

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You control a battlefield with a <Card name="Stalwart Poro" /> on it.
	Your opponent moves their <Card name="Ravenbloom Student" /> into that battlefield starting a showdown.
	Once your opponent gains focus, they play <Card name="Hextech Ray" /> targeting the Stalwart Poro which successfully resolves and kills it.
	The showdown *does not* end right away and you gain focus as usual.
	You still have the opportunity to play spells in an attempt to prevent your opponent from conquering.
</Callout>

## What happens when I move a unit into a non-combat showdown? [#non-combat-showdown]

When a unit controlled by a different player becomes present at the battlefield during a non-combat showdown, the showdown immediately converts into a combat showdown.<Rule number="316.8.b.1.a" />
This conversion happens during the cleanup at the end of the action that caused the unit to arrive.<Rule number="323.14" />

Once converted to a combat showdown, attacker and defender designations are assigned during the next cleanup,<Rule number="323.2" /> which in turn enables combat-related keywords (such as <Assault /> or <Shield />) to take effect and attack or defend triggers to happen.
The player who originally applied contested status to the battlefield becomes the attacker,<Rule number="464.2.c.1" /> and the player who moves in during the showdown becomes the defender.<Rule number="464.2.c.2" />

This is what the community calls the surprise defender.
Newer players often assume that the last player to enter a battlefield is the one attacking it, but the game revolves around whoever contests the battlefield first.
That player is the attacker, so the player joining afterwards is made a defender — frequently to their own surprise if they are not yet familiar with this rule.

## When do attack/defend triggers happen? [#attack-defend-triggers]

Attack and defend triggered abilities (such as "When I attack" or "When I defend") trigger when a unit receives its attacker or defender designation, not necessarily at the start of combat.
These designations are assigned during cleanups,<Rule number="323.2" /> which means units don't need to be present at the beginning of combat to get their triggers.

### Units can join combat late and still trigger

If a unit is moved into a battlefield where combat is already in progress, it will receive the appropriate attacker or defender designation during the next cleanup.<Rule number="323.2.a" />
When it receives this designation, any "When I attack" or "When I defend" abilities will trigger at that time.

### Attack/Defend abilities only trigger once per combat

Attack and defend triggered abilities can only trigger once per combat, even if a unit gains and loses its designation multiple times.<Rule number="383.4.e.2.a" /><Rule number="383.4.f.2.a" />

Once a unit has been assigned an attacker or defender designation during a combat, moving it away from the battlefield will cause it to lose that designation.<Rule number="323.2.c" />
If that unit is moved back to the same battlefield during the same combat, it will gain the same designation as its controller,<Rule number="323.2.a" /> which will be the same designation it previously had.
However, its "When I attack" or "When I defend" ability will not trigger again, as these abilities can only be fulfilled once per combat.
