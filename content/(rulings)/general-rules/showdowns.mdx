## When does a showdown close? [#showdown-close]

Under normal game procedure, a showdown does not close abruptly.
It ends once all players have consecutively passed focus on an empty chain.<Rule number="348" />

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You control a battlefield with a 2 might <Card name="Stalwart Poro" /> on it.
	Your opponent moves their <Card name="Ravenbloom Student" /> into that battlefield starting a showdown.
	When combat opens, Stalwart Poro becomes a defender and its <Shield /> increases its might to 3.
	Once your opponent gains focus, they play <Card name="Hextech Ray" /> targeting Stalwart Poro.
	Hextech Ray resolves, marking 3 damage on it.
	During the resulting cleanup, Stalwart Poro is killed for lethal damage.<Rule number="323.5" />
	The showdown *does not* end right away and you gain focus as usual.
	You still have the opportunity to play a legally timed card or ability in an attempt to prevent your opponent from conquering.<Rule number="347.1" />
</Callout>

## What happens when I move a unit into a non-combat showdown? [#non-combat-showdown]

When a unit controlled by a different player becomes present during a non-combat showdown, combat is staged and the showdown becomes a combat showdown during the cleanup following that unit's arrival.<Rule number="316.8.b.1.a" /><Rule number="323.9" /><Rule number="323.14" />

As combat opens, the player who originally applied contested status to the battlefield becomes the attacker,<Rule number="464.2.c.1" /> and the player who moves in during the showdown becomes the defender.<Rule number="464.2.c.2" />
Their units at that battlefield receive the corresponding designations then,<Rule number="464.2.c.3" /> enabling combat-related keywords such as <Assault /> and <Shield /> and causing attack or defend triggers to happen.

This interaction is commonly called the surprise defender: the player who first contested the battlefield remains the attacker, while the player joining later becomes the defender.

## When do attack/defend triggers happen? [#attack-defend-triggers]

Attack and defend triggered abilities (such as "When I attack" or "When I defend") trigger when a unit receives its attacker or defender designation, not necessarily at the start of combat.
Units already present receive their initial designations when combat opens.<Rule number="464.2.c.3" />
Units that join an ongoing combat receive their designations during the cleanup following their arrival.

### Units can join combat late and still trigger [#late-combat-triggers]

If a unit is moved into a battlefield where combat is already in progress, it will receive the appropriate attacker or defender designation during the next cleanup.<Rule number="323.2.a" />
When it receives this designation, any "When I attack" or "When I defend" abilities will trigger at that time.

### Attack and defend abilities only trigger once per combat [#once-per-combat-triggers]

Attack and defend triggered abilities can only trigger once per combat, even if a unit gains and loses its designation multiple times.<Rule number="383.4.e.2.a" /><Rule number="383.4.f.2.a" />

Once a unit has been assigned an attacker or defender designation during a combat, moving it away from the battlefield will cause it to lose that designation.<Rule number="323.2.c" />
If that unit is moved back to the same battlefield during the same combat, it will gain the same designation as its controller,<Rule number="323.2.a" /> which will be the same designation it previously had.
However, its "When I attack" or "When I defend" ability will not trigger again, as these abilities can only be fulfilled once per combat.
