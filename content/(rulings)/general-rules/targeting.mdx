## What makes something a target? [#target-definition]

Unless one of the exceptions below applies, a game object, player, or zone mentioned by a spell or ability is a target.<Rule number="355.7" /><Rule number="355.10" />
All targets are declared during finalization.<Rule number="355.8" />

Card text does not need to use the word "target" or "choose" explicitly — targeting can be implied, as in "Kill a unit" or "Move a friendly unit."
Abilities that reference "when you choose" a game object, such as <Card name="Irelia, Fervent" />'s "When you choose or ready me, give me +1 Might this turn," are specifically triggered when being targeted.<Rule number="383.4.b.3" />

When in doubt, assume it is a target — the list below covers the only situations where it is not:

- **It is in a non-public zone** (hand, main deck, etc.)<Rule number="355.10.a" /> e.g., "You may play a unit from your hand" does not target a unit — it merely lets you play one.
- **It is only a location requirement for another target**.<Rule number="355.10.b" />
Words like "at a battlefield" narrow down which objects are valid targets; the battlefield itself is not a target — unless the effect lets you choose *which* of several battlefields to affect, as in "Kill all units at a battlefield."
- **It is part of a trigger condition, cost, or replacement effect**.<Rule number="355.10.c" /><Rule number="355.10.c.1" />
Objects referenced in "when X happens" conditions, payment costs (see also [When do I pay a cost in a triggered ability that says "you may"?](/general-rules/abilities#costs-within-instructions)), or "instead" effects are not targets.
e.g., the dying unit in "When a friendly unit dies, kill a gear" is not a target — only the gear is.
- **There is no real choice involved**.<Rule number="355.10.d" />
If an effect applies to every object meeting a description rather than a specific one you pick, nothing is targeted.
e.g., "Kill all units at battlefields" targets nothing because you are not choosing among individual units.
This applies only when a choice is *never* possible — not when an object merely happens to be the only valid choice right now: "Kill a unit at a battlefield" still targets a unit even if only one unit is there.<Rule number="355.10.d.1" /><Rule number="355.10.d.2" />
- **It is part of a set chosen wholly or partly by other players**.<Rule number="355.10.e" />
Objects chosen this way are not targets, even if the spell or ability's controller also chooses part of the set.
e.g., "Each player kills a unit they control" targets nothing — each player chooses their unit as the effect resolves.
- **The instruction uses "must"**.<Rule number="355.10.f" />
This signals the choice is made on resolution rather than upfront.
e.g., "You must recycle one of your runes" does not target, whereas "Recycle a rune you control" does.

## Does "Kill a unit if it has 3 might or less" mean I can only choose a unit with 3 might or less? [#target-restrictions]

No.
Only words that describe the target restrict which objects can be chosen.

For example, "Kill a unit with 3 might or less" can target only a unit meeting that description.<Rule number="355.9.b" />

A separate condition on an effect does not restrict its targets.
"Kill a unit if it has 3 might or less" can target any otherwise legal unit; the might condition is checked when that instruction resolves.

## What happens if a target becomes illegal before a spell or ability resolves? [#illegal-targets]

The illegal target is unaffected, but the spell or ability still resolves.
Instructions related to that target cannot be followed, while independent instructions continue normally.<Rule number="359.3.e.5" /><Rule number="359.3.e.6" />

If an instruction needs information from the illegal target, that information is null and calculations using it are ignored.<Rule number="359.3.e.12" />
If a later instruction is linked to an earlier instruction that was ignored, the later instruction is also ignored.<Rule number="359.3.e.14.a" />
