## Are paid costs refunded if a card or ability is countered? [#countered-costs]

No.
Countering does not refund any costs paid to play a card, activate an ability, or trigger an ability, including additional costs.<Rule number="425.1.c" /><Rule number="425.1.c.1" />

A countered card or ability does nothing and is cleared from the chain,<Rule number="425.1.a" /> but anything already spent, discarded, killed, or otherwise paid as a cost remains paid.

## What are optional additional costs? [#optional-additional-costs]

Optional additional costs are additional costs a player may choose to pay.<Rule number="356.2.b.1" />

Their rules text uses both "may" and the exact phrase "as an additional cost."
<Accelerate /> and <Repeat /> are keyworded optional additional costs.<Rule number="805.1.a" /><Rule number="820.1.d" />

The player chooses whether to pay an optional additional cost while making relevant choices.
If chosen, it is included while determining the card's total cost before applicable discounts are applied.<Rule number="355.1.a" /><Rule number="356.2" /><Rule number="356.4" />

## What costs still apply when I play a card ignoring its cost? [#ignored-costs]

Additional costs and cost increases still apply.
Playing a card "ignoring its cost" sets only its base energy and power costs to zero.<Rule number="356.1.b.1" />
Additional costs and cost increases are applied afterward and can raise its total cost above zero.<Rule number="356.1.b.3" />

Mandatory additional costs must still be paid, while optional additional costs may still be chosen and paid.<Rule number="356.2.a.1" /><Rule number="356.2.b.1" />
By contrast, "ignoring any and all costs" sets the card's total cost to zero, including non-standard costs.<Rule number="356.5.a" />

## Can players react while costs are being paid? [#cost-reaction-window]

Not through a normal reaction window.
Costs are paid while the card or ability is being finalized, and finalization does not pass priority.<Rule number="357" /><Rule number="404" /><Rule number="337.1.a" />

When resources are required, the player paying them may activate eligible <Add /> abilities with <Reaction /> during that payment.<Rule number="429.3" /><Rule number="444.2.c" />
This is a special permission for resource payment, not a normal reaction window: those abilities finalize and resolve immediately without passing priority.<Rule number="429.2" /><Rule number="429.2.a" />

Any normal opportunity to react comes only after finalization, when the costs have already been paid.
