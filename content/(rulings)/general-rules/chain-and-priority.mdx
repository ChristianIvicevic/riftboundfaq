## Can I react to units being played? [#unit-play-reactions]

No, as units enter the board immediately without priority being passed to other players.

Unlike spells, units resolve the moment they are finalized and do not open a window of opportunity for reactions.<Rule number="337.2" /><Rule number="359.2" />

If the unit has a play effect ("When you play me…"), or if another permanent's triggered ability fires as it enters the board, those triggered abilities are placed on the chain as pending items *after* the unit has already entered.<Rule number="383.4.a.2" /><Rule number="383.2.c" /><Rule number="383.3" />
Once those pending items are finalized with no further items pending, priority passes and players may react<Rule number="337.4" /> — but by that point, the unit is already in play.

## What happens when multiple items are pending on the chain? [#pending-item-order]

Pending items are finalized in the order they were added, starting with the oldest.<Rule number="337.1" /><Rule number="337.1.b" />
A unit, gear, or ability that adds resources resolves immediately when it finalizes.<Rule number="337.2" />
Other finalized items remain on the chain while the remaining pending items are processed.

Players do not receive priority until no pending items remain.<Rule number="337.3" /><Rule number="337.4" />
After players pass priority, finalized chain items resolve in the opposite direction, starting with the newest.<Rule number="340.1" />

## Can I play a reaction after one, but not all, items on the chain have resolved? [#partial-chain-reactions]

Yes, once you have gained priority.
The chain does not have to resolve in its entirety prior to (re)gaining priority, unlike in some other TCGs.

Any time the most recent item on the chain resolves and there are no pending items, the controller of the newest item on the chain gains priority.<Rule number="340.4" />

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You put <Card name="Singularity" /> on the chain targeting an opponent's unit.
	Your opponent reacts by putting their <Card name="Wind Wall" /> on the chain, attempting to counter the Singularity.
	In response, you play <Card name="Stupefy" />, in the hopes of drawing into a counterspell of your own.
	Once Stupefy resolves and you draw a card, priority is back to your opponent as the most recent item is their Wind Wall.
	After they pass priority, you can react once again and play your own counterspell, assuming you drew it off of the Stupefy.
</Callout>

## Do I lose control of a battlefield as soon as my last unit leaves? [#empty-battlefield-control]

No, not immediately.

Outside a showdown, control of an empty battlefield is checked during a cleanup, not at the moment the last unit leaves.
You lose control only if the battlefield is still empty and the turn is in an open state.<Rule number="190.4.c" /><Rule number="323.6" />

If the chain still contains an item when a cleanup occurs, the turn is in a closed state and you keep control through that cleanup.<Rule number="309.1" />
You can lose control during a later cleanup after the turn returns to an open state if the battlefield remains empty.
