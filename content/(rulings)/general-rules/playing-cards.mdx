## What does "play" mean on a card? [#play-definition]

"Play" refers to the same underlying action, but rules text uses it in three contexts that initiate or check that action at different points.<Rule number="419.1" /><Rule number="419.4" />

In trigger conditions — "When you play," as seen on <Card name="Ravenbloom Student" /> and <Card name="Darius, Trifarian" /> — it means resolve.
These abilities trigger once playing the card is complete, which happens at its resolution.<Rule number="419.4" /><Rule number="419.4.a" />
For units and gear, that's the instant they're finalized, since both resolve immediately without priority being passed;<Rule number="337.2" /> for spells, resolution is a separate, later step, reached only after finalization and any reactions have played out.<Rule number="339.1" /><Rule number="340.1" />

In conditional statements checking whether a card has been played — "if you've played," as seen on <Card name="Battering Ram" /> and <Card name="Crescent Guardian" /> — it means finalize, not resolve.
A card satisfies this check when it becomes a finalized chain item.<Rule number="329.3" />
The <Legion /> keyword makes this precise: it's written as "if you have played another card this turn,"<Rule number="812.1.b.1" /> but the dependent ability is active as long as that other card "has been Finalized by you on the same turn" — not resolved.<Rule number="812.1.c" />

As an instruction — "play a card," as seen on <Card name="Here to Help" /> and <Card name="Fizz, Trickster" /> — it initiates that underlying action by placing the card on the chain and queuing it for finalization.<Rule number="419.1" />

## When I play a card from my trash, is it still in my trash? [#playing-from-trash]

No.
The first step of playing a card moves it from its current zone to the chain, where it waits to be finalized.<Rule number="354" /><Rule number="354.2" />

Once you begin playing a card from your trash, it is no longer there while you make choices, determine its total cost, or resolve it.
If the card counts copies of itself in your trash, only the other copies still there count.
Likewise, if its cost is reduced for each card in your trash, it does not count itself toward that reduction.
