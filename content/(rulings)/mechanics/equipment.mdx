## What happens to attached equipment when a unit dies? [#equipment-after-death]

The equipment detaches and remains at the unit's last location, recalling if at a battlefield.

When a unit with attached equipment dies, the equipment detaches and remains at the location the unit occupied before dying.<Rule number="719.5" /><Rule number="435.4.b" />

If that location is a battlefield, the detached equipment will be recalled to its controller's base during the next cleanup.<Rule number="435.4.a" />
If that location is the controller's base, the equipment simply remains there.

## What happens to attached equipment when you take control of an enemy unit? [#equipment-control-change]

Equipment stays attached but its controller does not change — only the top-most unit's controller changes.

When you take control of a unit, any equipment attached to it retains its current controller.
Changes in control of the top-most card do not affect control of attached cards, and vice versa.<Rule number="718.5.f" />

When the equipment detaches, it remains at the unit's last location.<Rule number="719.5" /><Rule number="435.4.b" />
If that location is a battlefield, it is recalled to its controller's base during the next cleanup.<Rule number="435.4.a" />
If that location is another player's base, the equipment remains there until the next cleanup, when it is recalled to its controller's base.<Rule number="323.7" />

Because the equipment remains under the other player's control, that player can play a unit with <Weaponmaster /> and choose the equipment, even while it is attached to the unit you control.<Rule number="718.5.b" /><Rule number="821.1.c" />
If its <Equip /> cost can be paid and it can legally be detached and attached, it moves to the newly played unit.<Rule number="821.1.c.4" /><Rule number="821.1.c.5" />

## Which equipment effects choose units? [#equipment-unit-selection]

<Equip /> and <QuickDraw /> choose units; <Weaponmaster /> does not.

Some abilities trigger or care about when you choose friendly units. Here's how equipment-related effects interact with such abilities:

- When you activate a gear's <Equip /> ability, you choose a unit you control to attach the equipment to.<Rule number="818.1.b.1" />
This choice is a target, so activating <Equip /> counts as choosing a unit.<Rule number="355.7" />
- When you play a gear with <QuickDraw />, its triggered ability instructs you to "attach it to a Unit you control".<Rule number="819.1.d" />
While attaching cards doesn't inherently choose or specify a target,<Rule number="434.3" /> <QuickDraw />'s ability does require you to choose which unit to attach to.<Rule number="355.10" />
This counts as choosing a unit.
- When you play a unit with <Weaponmaster />, you choose an equipment you control, not the unit.<Rule number="821.1.c" />
The rules explicitly state that "The <Equip /> ability is not activated this way, and the unit with the <Weaponmaster /> ability is not chosen".<Rule number="821.1.c.6" />
Therefore, <Weaponmaster /> does **not** count as choosing a unit.

## Can I react to equipment abilities? [#equipment-reactions]

Yes — all three equipment-related abilities create chain items that opponents can respond to before the attachment resolves.

<Equip /> is an activated ability.<Rule number="818.1" />
Activated abilities use the chain,<Rule number="377.3" /> so when you activate <Equip />, opponents have an opportunity to play a <Reaction /> spell or ability before the attachment resolves.<Rule number="377.3.b.2" />

When you play a <QuickDraw /> gear, the gear itself resolves immediately when finalized and cannot be reacted to.<Rule number="337.2" />
However, <QuickDraw />'s triggered ability — "When you play this, attach it to a unit you control"<Rule number="819.1.d" /> — is placed on the chain as a separate item.<Rule number="383.4.a.2" />
Opponents can respond to that ability before the attachment resolves.

<Weaponmaster /> is also a triggered ability keyword.<Rule number="821.1" />
Its ability triggers when you play the unit, and is placed on the chain just like any other triggered ability.<Rule number="383.4.a.2" />
Opponents can respond to it before the equipment is attached.

Skipping straight to attaching without checking for reactions is not correct play for any of these abilities.
