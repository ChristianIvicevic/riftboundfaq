## When do I decide whether to pay for Repeat? [#repeat-decision-timing]

You must decide whether to pay a spell's <Repeat /> cost when you play the spell, not later.

When you play a spell with <Repeat />, you follow the normal process for playing a card:<Rule number="353" />
1. First, you put the spell onto the chain<Rule number="354" />
2. Then, you make relevant choices,<Rule number="355" /> including whether or not you will pay its <Repeat /> cost<Rule number="355.1" /><Rule number="820.1.d" />
3. Next, you determine its total cost.<Rule number="356" />
If you chose to pay it, you apply the <Repeat /> cost as an optional additional cost.<Rule number="356.2.b.1" /><Rule number="820.1.d" />
4. Finally, you complete the remaining steps of playing the card to finalize the spell

This means you cannot put a spell on the chain, pass priority to see what happens, and then decide whether to pay its <Repeat /> cost later.
The decision must be made upfront during finalization.
Any targets, modes, or other choices for the additional execution are also made during finalization and may differ from the original execution's choices.<Rule number="820.2" /><Rule number="820.2.a" />

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You put <Card name="Blood Rush" />, a spell with <Repeat /> <Energy value={1} />, that says "Give a unit <Assault value={2} /> this turn," onto the chain.
	Before anyone can respond, you must decide whether to pay the <Energy value={1} /> <Repeat /> cost and choose the target for each execution.
	The two targets may be the same unit or different units.
	Once you've made this choice and paid costs, the spell is finalized.
	Your opponent can now respond with priority, but your <Repeat /> decision is locked in.
</Callout>

## What happens if a repeated spell is countered? [#repeated-spell-countering]

The entire spell is countered, including all repeated instructions.

<Repeat /> does *not* create a second instance of the spell on the chain.<Rule number="820.3.a" />
Instead, it modifies how the spell resolves by executing its instructions an additional time.<Rule number="820.1.d.1" />

When a spell is countered, it does nothing and is cleared from the chain.<Rule number="425.1.a" />
Since the spell never resolves, none of its instructions are executed, whether they came from <Repeat /> or not.
