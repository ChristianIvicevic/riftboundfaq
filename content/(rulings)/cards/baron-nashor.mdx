## Does Baron Nashor move to the Baron Pit when it's created as part of playing him? [#creation-movement]

No.
Baron Nashor enters the <Card name="Baron Pit" /> directly — his entry location is replaced, not his position on the board.
A move is defined as a permanent changing position from one space on the board to another.<Rule number="446.1" />
Baron Nashor is entering the board for the first time (a zone change), which is not a move.<Rule number="446.2" />

## What happens after Baron Nashor enters the Baron Pit? [#baron-pit-showdown]

A non-combat showdown opens during the resulting cleanup.

### Resolution Sequence [#baron-pit-showdown-sequence]

1. The <Card name="Baron Pit" /> is newly created and has no controller when Baron Nashor enters it.<Rule number="439.4.b" />
2. Baron Nashor entering the uncontrolled battlefield applies contested status to it.<Rule number="190.3.a.1" />
3. During the resulting cleanup, a non-combat showdown is marked as staged at the Baron Pit.<Rule number="316.8.b" /><Rule number="323.8" />
4. Later in that cleanup, the staged showdown opens.<Rule number="323.12" /><Rule number="344.2" />

If an opposing unit arrives at the Baron Pit during the showdown, a combat is staged and the non-combat showdown becomes a combat showdown during the following cleanup.<Rule number="316.8.b.1.a" /><Rule number="323.9" /><Rule number="323.14" />

If the showdown closes with only Baron Nashor's controller's units present at the Baron Pit, and they have not yet scored it this turn, they conquer it and score.<Rule number="348.2.a" /><Rule number="348.2.a.1" />

## Does the Baron Pit need to be scored to win by conquering? [#baron-pit-victory]

Yes.
Winning by conquering at match point requires having scored every battlefield on the board that turn — including the Baron Pit.<Rule number="471.1.b.1" />
If it hasn't been scored, the winning conquer draws a card instead.

Winning by hold is unaffected, as it has no such requirement.<Rule number="471.1.a.1" />

## Can I play Baron Nashor while an opponent's Mageseeker Warden is at a battlefield? [#warden-play-lock]

It depends on whether the <Card name="Baron Pit" /> is already on the board.

**If the Baron Pit is not yet on the board**, Baron Nashor enters the Baron Pit, not your base.
As Baron Nashor is played, his "as you play me" instruction fires during the playing process — the Baron Pit battlefield token is added to the board.<Rule number="135.2.b.3" /><Rule number="187.9" />
Under <Card name="Mageseeker Warden" />'s restriction, your base is the only valid playing location — where Baron Nashor is nominally played to — but his replacement effect overrides where he actually enters.<Rule number="355.2.a" />
His "If you do, I enter there" then functions as a replacement effect that applies as he enters the board, replacing his entry at his original play location with entry at the Baron Pit.<Rule number="369.3" /><Rule number="370.1.b" />

**If the Baron Pit is already on the board**, the "as you play me" instruction does nothing — the Baron Pit token is only added if it's not there already — so the "If you do" condition is not met.
Baron Nashor simply enters his base, the only valid playing location under Mageseeker Warden's restriction.<Rule number="355.2.a" />
