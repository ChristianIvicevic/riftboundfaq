## If Gangplank replaces one part of a spell or ability, does the rest still resolve? [#partial-resolution]

Yes.
A replacement effect only replaces the specific event or instruction that qualifies for it.<Rule number="370.1" />
For Gangplank, that means a stun, might decrease, or return to hand caused by a spell or ability that chose him.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	A player plays <Card name="Star-Crossed" />, targeting their own <Empowered /> Gangplank as the friendly unit and an opponent's <Card name="Elder Dragon" /> as the enemy unit.

	Returning Elder Dragon doesn't choose Gangplank, so his replacement effect has nothing to apply to there — it's returned to hand as normal.
	Returning Gangplank is the qualifying event: instead of going to hand, he gets +3 might.
	The rest of Star-Crossed resolves normally; it simply produces a different result for the one instruction that involves Gangplank.
</Callout>

## What happens when Switcheroo would reduce an Empowered Gangplank's might? [#switcheroo-might-reduction]

The might reduction is replaced with +3 might, while the modifier applied to the other unit is unchanged.
See [How does Switcheroo work?](/cards/switcheroo#might-swap) for why swapping creates two separate modifiers from a single calculated difference.<Rule number="433.1.a" /><Rule number="433.1.b" />
Switcheroo chooses Gangplank when the spell is finalized, then creates one modifier for each unit when it resolves.
If the modifier affecting Gangplank would decrease his might, that event is replaced with +3 might because it comes from a spell that chose him.
If it would increase his might, it does not match the events his ability replaces and applies normally.
The modifier affecting the other unit is unchanged, and the difference is not recalculated.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	An <Empowered /> Gangplank at 6 might is swapped with a 1 might <Card name="Determined Sentry" /> at the same battlefield via <Card name="Switcheroo" />.

	The swap action calculates a difference of 5, then creates two modifiers: -5 might for Gangplank and +5 might for Determined Sentry.
	Gangplank's replacement effect intercedes on his -5 might modifier — decreasing his might is one of the events it replaces — and turns it into +3 might instead.
	The +5 might modifier applies only to Determined Sentry, so Gangplank's replacement effect does not affect it.

	Gangplank ends at 9 might (6 base + 3); Determined Sentry ends at 6 might (1 base + 5).
</Callout>
