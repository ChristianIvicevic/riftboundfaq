## Does Diana, Lunari's ability resolve before or after attack and defend abilities from the start of combat? [#trigger-resolution-order]

Diana's ability resolves after all attack and defend triggers generated as that combat opens.

When combat opens with a combat showdown, the attacker and defender designations are assigned to units.<Rule number="464.2.c.3" />
Attack triggers fire when a unit gains the attacker designation, and defend triggers fire when a unit gains the defender designation.<Rule number="383.4.e" /><Rule number="383.4.f" />
Those triggers are then added to the combat chain, with the attacking player placing theirs first, followed by others in turn order, and the defending player last.<Rule number="464.2.e" /><Rule number="464.2.e.1" />

Diana's "when a showdown begins here" trigger is a start-of-showdown effect, which the combat-opening sequence processes as its very first step — before designations are established and their attack and defend triggers are added to the chain.<Rule number="464.2.b" />
Because the chain resolves newest-item-first, Diana's ability resolves last — after every attack and defend trigger from that same combat chain has already resolved.<Rule number="340.1" />

## When do I pay the 1 energy for Diana, Lunari's ability? [#ability-finalization-cost]

You pay it while Diana's triggered ability is being finalized, before opponents can react.

<Callout type="warn" title="Outdated CR example">
	Diana received a functional errata alongside the Core Rules 1.4 update.
	Older printings say "you may pay <Energy value={1} />. If you do, ..."; her updated text says "you may pay <Energy value={1} /> to ..." instead.
	This changes the energy payment from an instruction performed during resolution into a cost paid while finalizing the ability.
	CR 444.2 still describes Diana using her pre-errata behavior and incorrectly places the payment on resolution.<Rule number="444.2" />
</Callout>

Because "you may" is the first part of the effect, you choose whether to perform the ability during finalization.<Rule number="383.3.a" />
The immediately following "pay <Energy value={1} /> to <Predict />" is a cost within instructions, so the payment is treated as the triggered ability's base cost and must be paid for the ability to finalize.<Rule number="383.3.b" /><Rule number="383.3.b.1" />
You may activate <Add /> reactions while paying this cost.<Rule number="444.2.c" />

If you do not pay, the ability does not finalize and none of its effects occur.
If you pay, the ability is placed on the chain; when it resolves, you <Predict />, reveal the top card of your main deck, and draw that card if it is a spell.
