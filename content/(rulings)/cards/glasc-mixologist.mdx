## Can Glasc Mixologist's Deathknell target a unit that died from the same event? [#same-event-target]

Yes, because targets are not chosen until the ability is finalized — by which point all units that died in the same event are already in the trash.

Whenever a unit with <Deathknell /> dies, its trigger is placed on the chain as a pending item.<Rule number="808.1.d.2" />
During a cleanup, this ordering is enforced system-wide: step 3a queues all <Deathknell /> triggers from every unit with lethal damage<Rule number="323.4" /> and only then does step 3b move those units to the trash.<Rule number="323.5" />

The same result applies when one kill instruction kills multiple units. Their deaths are simultaneous because they result from the same game action,<Rule number="370.1.a.2" /> and each relevant <Deathknell /> becomes pending before its unit moves to the trash.<Rule number="428.1.a.1.b" /><Rule number="808.1.d.2" />

In either case, all units that died from that event are in the trash before the pending ability is finalized and its target is chosen.<Rule number="334.2" /><Rule number="355.8" />

## Can Glasc Mixologist's Deathknell play a unit to the battlefield where he died? [#play-to-same-battlefield]

Yes, as long as you controlled that battlefield when Glasc Mixologist died.

**Outside a showdown**, the <Deathknell /> trigger remains on the chain as a pending item from the moment it is queued.
Because a chain exists, the turn remains in a closed state,<Rule number="309.1" /> and cleanup step 4 — which makes unoccupied battlefields uncontrolled — only applies during an open state.<Rule number="323.6" />
The battlefield stays under your control, so when the unit is finalized you can choose it as a valid destination.<Rule number="355.2.a" />

**During a showdown**, control of the battlefield cannot change until instructed by the steps of the combat or showdown,<Rule number="190.4.b" /> so the question does not arise.

## What happens when Glasc Mixologist is my only defender and his Deathknell plays a unit where he died? [#deathknell-combat-result]

Several things here commonly catch players off guard: surviving enemy units heal to full before the new unit arrives, neither player wins the original combat, and a second combat immediately follows.

The combat cleanup heals all units before any pending trigger can resolve,<Rule number="466.1.a.1" /><Rule number="334" /> so by the time Glasc's <Deathknell /> finalizes and the new unit reaches the battlefield, the enemy is already back to full health.
Because Glasc was your only defender, no defenders remain during cleanup step 3d, so the surviving attackers are not recalled.<Rule number="466.1.a.2" />

With units from both players now present, combat result is determined. Because both players have units there, the result is "No Result."<Rule number="466.3.d" />
A fresh combat is immediately staged at that battlefield.<Rule number="466.3.d.1" />
