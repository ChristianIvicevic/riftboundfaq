## Can Temporal Breach banish a unit at a different battlefield if played from Hidden? [#hidden-targeting-restriction]

No, when Temporal Breach is played from Hidden, the unit it banishes must be chosen from among units at the battlefield where it was hidden.

"Banish a unit" chooses a specific game object to affect, making it a target, declared as Temporal Breach is put on the chain.<Rule number="355.5" /><Rule number="355.7" /><Rule number="355.8" />
Choices made while playing a card from Hidden are restricted to the battlefield where it was hidden.<Rule number="811.1.d" />
Specifically, if a Hidden spell chooses any targets, those targets must be chosen from among options at that battlefield, unless the ability explicitly restricts targeting in a way that makes this impossible.<Rule number="811.1.d.2" />
Temporal Breach's own wording doesn't restrict its target to a location, so this Hidden restriction applies in full — a unit at another battlefield, or in base, isn't a legal target when it's played from Hidden.

If Temporal Breach is played normally, the Hidden restriction does not apply.
It may target an otherwise legal unit at any board location, including a base or battlefield.<Rule number="811.3" />

## Can I banish my only unit at a battlefield I control and replay it there without losing control in between? [#battlefield-control]

Yes, control of the battlefield is never lost during this sequence.

When Temporal Breach resolves, the replayed unit is added to the chain as a pending item,<Rule number="354.2" /> with its play process held until the spell has fully resolved.<Rule number="354.3" />
Once the spell finishes resolving, a cleanup runs — but the pending unit keeps the chain alive throughout it.<Rule number="330" /><Rule number="331" />
Because the chain is not empty, the turn remains in a closed state, and cleanup step 4 — which would make the battlefield uncontrolled — only applies during an open state.<Rule number="323.6" />
Control is never lost, so any effects that care about gaining or losing control of that battlefield don't trigger.
When all outstanding tasks are complete, the pending unit is finalized<Rule number="334.2" /><Rule number="337.1" /> and replayed to the same battlefield you still control, as dictated by Temporal Breach's own text.<Rule number="359.2" />

## What happens if Temporal Breach banishes a unit at Rockfall Path? [#rockfall-path]

Temporal Breach banishes the unit, but its owner cannot play it back to <Card name="Rockfall Path" />.
The unit remains in its owner's banishment.

Rockfall Path prevents the instructed play action.
This does not stop Temporal Breach from resolving; when it reaches the replay instruction, that game action is skipped as impossible.<Rule number="358.3.a" />
The earlier banish instruction is unaffected, so the unit remains banished.

## What happens if Temporal Breach banishes a token unit? [#token-unit]

The token stops existing the moment it's banished, so there's nothing left for its owner to replay.

Temporal Breach puts the token into its owner's banishment, which is a non-board zone.<Rule number="056.1" />
Tokens can only exist on the board or the chain — if a token enters any other zone, it ceases to exist immediately upon arriving there.<Rule number="186" /><Rule number="186.1" />
So by the time Temporal Breach's "then its owner plays it to the same location" instruction would apply, the token is already gone, and that part of the spell does nothing for it.<Rule number="055" />

## Can I pay optional additional costs, like Accelerate, when replaying the banished unit? [#optional-additional-costs]

Yes, since the replayed unit still goes through the complete, normal process of playing a card, and "ignoring its cost" only narrows one part of that process — the base cost — leaving everything else, including optional additional costs, unaffected.

"Its owner plays it" instructs the normal process of playing a card — the unit is removed from banishment and put onto the chain as a pending item,<Rule number="354" /><Rule number="354.2" /> it isn't placed directly onto the board.
That pending item goes through the full finalization process once it's ready to be finalized, including making relevant choices<Rule number="355" /> and determining total cost.<Rule number="356" />
Playing a card "ignoring its cost" only sets its base Energy and base Power cost to zero;<Rule number="356.1.b.1" /> further additional costs applied in later steps can still raise the total cost back above zero.<Rule number="356.1.b.3" />
<Accelerate /> is one such optional additional cost, paid as a player plays the unit with the ability,<Rule number="805.2" /> so its owner may still declare their intent to pay it during finalization<Rule number="356.2.b.1" /> and pay it as usual, unaffected by Temporal Breach ignoring the unit's base cost.
