## Does the target's controller draw if the kill is replaced? [#draw-if-not-killed]

Yes.
Replacing Hidden Blade's kill does not prevent its linked draw instruction from executing.

The phrase "its controller" links the draw to the unit affected by the preceding kill instruction.<Rule number="359.3.e.14" />
[Replacing the kill does not stop a later instruction that refers only to the affected object](/general-rules/replacement-effects#partial-resolution).
Hidden Blade's draw references the targeted unit's controller, not whether it was killed, so the draw still executes.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You play Hidden Blade targeting an opponent's unit.
	They respond with <Card name="Zhonya's Hourglass" />, which replaces the unit's death.
	The unit is not killed.
	Because it was still a legal target when Hidden Blade resolved, the opponent draws 2.
</Callout>

## Does the controller draw if the target becomes illegal? [#draw-if-illegal]

No.
An illegal target is entirely unaffected by the spell, and the draw is no exception.

When Hidden Blade's target becomes illegal, [the kill instruction and its linked draw instruction are ignored](/general-rules/targeting#illegal-targets), so the controller does not draw.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You play Hidden Blade targeting an opponent's unit at a battlefield.
	They respond by moving it to their base using <Card name="Flash" />.
	Hidden Blade resolves.
	The unit is no longer at a battlefield, so it is an illegal target.
	The unit is not killed and the opponent does not draw.
</Callout>
