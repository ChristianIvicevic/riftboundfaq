## How does Emperor's Dais's ability work? [#ability-finalization-cost]

When you conquer Emperor's Dais, you may pay <Energy value={1} /> and return a unit you control there to its owner's hand to finalize the triggered ability onto the chain.

<Callout type="info" title="Core Rules 1.4 errata">
	Emperor's Dais received a functional errata alongside the Core Rules 1.4 update.
	Older printings separate "pay <Energy value={1} /> and return a unit" from "If you do, play a 2 Might Sand Soldier unit token"; its updated text joins them using "to" instead.
	This makes the energy payment and unit return parts of a single cost performed while finalizing the ability, so the unit can no longer be returned without also paying the energy.
</Callout>

Because "you may" is the first part of the effect, you choose whether to perform the ability during finalization.<Rule number="383.3.a" />
The immediately following "pay <Energy value={1} /> and return a unit ... to play" is a cost within instructions, so both actions form the triggered ability's base cost and must be completed for the ability to finalize.<Rule number="383.3.b" /><Rule number="383.3.b.1" /><Rule number="404.1" />
You may activate <Add /> reactions while paying the energy portion of this cost.<Rule number="444.2.c" />

Returning the unit is part of the cost, so that unit is not a target and returns to its owner's hand during finalization rather than resolution.<Rule number="355.10.c.1" />
If you cannot or choose not to pay the entire cost, the ability does not finalize; you cannot return the unit without also paying the energy.<Rule number="404.2" />
If you complete the cost, the ability is placed on the chain; when it resolves, you play a 2 Might Sand Soldier unit token at Emperor's Dais.
