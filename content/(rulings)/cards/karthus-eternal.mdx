## Does Karthus, Eternal's ability apply if he dies from the same effect as the unit with Deathknell? [#simultaneous-deathknell]

Yes, because <Deathknell /> triggers are always queued as pending items before the dying unit leaves the board.

Whenever a unit with <Deathknell /> is killed — whether by a kill effect or by lethal damage during cleanup — its trigger is added to the chain before the card is moved to the trash.<Rule number="808.1.d.2" />
Karthus's passive is only active while he is on the board.<Rule number="365.1" />
When one kill action kills Karthus and another unit simultaneously, as with <Card name="The Ruination" />, Karthus is still present when that unit's <Deathknell /> triggers.<Rule number="370.1.a.2" />
In the cleanup case this ordering is enforced explicitly: step 3a queues all <Deathknell /> triggers<Rule number="323.4" /> before step 3b moves any of those units to the trash.<Rule number="323.5" />

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You control Karthus and a friendly unit with <Deathknell />.
	Your opponent plays <Card name="The Ruination" />, which kills all units.
	Both are sent to the trash, but not before Karthus's ability doubles the <Deathknell /> trigger.
</Callout>

## Does a single Not So Fast counter both Deathknell triggers when Karthus, Eternal is on the board? [#countering-dual-deathknells]

No.
If both <Deathknell /> triggers are legal targets for <Card name="Not So Fast" />, one copy can counter only one of them.

When a friendly unit with <Deathknell /> dies while Karthus is on the board, his ability causes that <Deathknell /> to trigger an additional time.
Each trigger is added to the chain as its own independent pending item.<Rule number="808.1.d.2" />
Not So Fast targets a single spell or ability, so each copy can counter only one trigger.

This applies only when the <Deathknell /> ability chooses one of your friendly units or gear.
Otherwise, Not So Fast cannot target either trigger.
