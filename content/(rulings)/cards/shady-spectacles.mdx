## Does a Reflection token with Temporary lose Temporary after Shady Spectacles makes it a copy of a unit that doesn't have Temporary? [#copying-temporary]

No. It keeps the <Temporary /> keyword granted to it separately, even if Shady Spectacles makes it a copy of a unit without <Temporary />.

<Card name="Deceiver" /> and <Card name="Mirror Image" /> first play a Reflection token, then make it a copy, and then grant it <Temporary />.
The keyword therefore comes from a separate effect rather than from the traits copied by the token.

Shady Spectacles' "becomes a copy of that unit" applies in the trait-altering layer,<Rule number="477.1.b" /><Rule number="477.1.b.1" /> while the separate keyword grant applies afterward in the ability-altering layer.<Rule number="477.2" /><Rule number="477.2.a" />
The rules' Reflection example likewise confirms that copied Reflection tokens retain <Temporary />.<Rule number="477.1.b.1.b" />
Because the grant has no stated duration, it continues for as long as the token remains on the board.<Rule number="801.3.a.3" />
