## Does Astral Heron's ability trigger if it's your first card played to a battlefield this turn? [#self-trigger]

Yes — assuming it's played to a battlefield.

Play, in a trigger condition, refers to a card resolving, not merely being placed on the chain.<Rule number="350.1" /><Rule number="419.4.a" />
Units resolve immediately after they finalize.<Rule number="337.2" />
During that resolution, the unit becomes a game object, its passive abilities become active, and it enters the board at its chosen location.<Rule number="359.2" /><Rule number="359.2.a" /><Rule number="359.2.c" />

A trigger's condition is evaluated after a potentially inciting event has been processed, and nothing requires that event to have been caused by a *different* card.<Rule number="383.2.c" /><Rule number="383.2.c.1" />
"When you play your first card each turn" begins the trigger condition,<Rule number="383.2.a" /> and "if I'm at a battlefield" immediately follows it, so that additional requirement is also part of the trigger condition and is checked when the condition is fulfilled.<Rule number="383.2.a.1" />
After Astral Heron finishes resolving, play-trigger conditions are evaluated.<Rule number="419.4.a" />
If it's the first card played this turn, Astral Heron is already at the battlefield when its condition is checked, so its ability is placed on the chain.

## Does Astral Heron trigger if your first card moves it to base? [#moved-during-resolution]

No.
If a spell such as <Card name="Flash" /> or <Card name="Emperor's Divide" /> moves Astral Heron to base while resolving, Astral Heron is no longer at a battlefield when its trigger condition is checked.

A card is not considered played, and abilities that trigger from playing it are not evaluated, until that card finishes resolving.<Rule number="350.1" /><Rule number="419.4.a" />
Astral Heron's trigger condition is therefore checked only after the spell's movement instructions have been completed.<Rule number="383.2.c" />
Because "if I'm at a battlefield" is part of that trigger condition, the ability is not placed on the chain if Astral Heron is at base by then.<Rule number="383.2.a.1" />

This differs from moving Astral Heron after its triggered ability has already been placed on the chain.
At that point, the condition has already been satisfied.

## Once Astral Heron's triggered ability is on the chain, can killing or moving it stop the cost reduction? [#removed-in-response]

No.
Once the triggered ability is on the chain, killing or moving Astral Heron does not stop the discount from applying to your next card.

"If I'm at a battlefield" is part of the trigger condition, so it's only checked when the triggering event occurs to decide whether the ability is placed on the chain — it is not re-checked when the ability later resolves.<Rule number="383.2.a.1" />
Nothing in the effect references Astral Heron's location or continued presence, so removing it does not stop the trigger from creating the discount.
Once created, that discount is a delayed ability and executes at its specified time regardless of whether Astral Heron remains on the board.<Rule number="392" />

## If the spell that receives Astral Heron's discount is later countered, is the discount granted to a different card instead? [#discount-after-counter]

No — the discount is spent the moment it reduces that spell's cost.

Astral Heron's triggered ability resolves into a delayed passive ability, active only for "your next card" — a single specified window, not a standing effect.<Rule number="390.4" /><Rule number="391" />
That window belongs to finalizing the next card, not to that card resolving successfully — it doesn't linger to see whether the spell goes on to succeed.
Discounts are applied while determining a card's total cost, in step 3 of the play process, and the reduced cost is paid in step 4 — both steps happen well before the card is even finalized, let alone placed on the chain where it could be countered.<Rule number="356.4" /><Rule number="356.4.a" /><Rule number="357" />
Whichever card is played next consumes that window the moment its cost is reduced; nothing that happens to that card afterward can reopen it.

A countered card does nothing and is not considered to have been played,<Rule number="425.1.a" /><Rule number="425.1.b" /> and countering does not refund any costs paid to play it, additional costs included.<Rule number="425.1.c" /><Rule number="425.1.c.1" />
That confirms the reduced cost itself stays spent — but Astral Heron's discount was already used up before the spell was even vulnerable to being countered, so there was never anything left to give to a different card.
