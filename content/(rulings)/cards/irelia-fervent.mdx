## If I target Irelia, Fervent with a spell or ability and it gets countered, does she still get +1 might? [#targeting-before-counter]

Yes.

Irelia is chosen as a target during step 2 of playing the spell or ability — the "Make relevant choices" step.<Rule number="355.5" /><Rule number="355.7" />
This happens before the spell or ability is finalized on the chain and before opponents have any opportunity to react.
After the spell or ability finishes finalizing, her targeting effect is added to the chain as a pending item.<Rule number="383.4.b.2" /><Rule number="383.4.b.3" />
That trigger is finalized before opponents receive an opportunity to react.<Rule number="337.3" /><Rule number="337.4" />
The trigger is not contingent on the spell or ability that caused it successfully resolving.
Countering the spell or ability only prevents it from resolving<Rule number="425.1.a" /> — it has no bearing on Irelia's already-triggered ability.

<Callout type="info">
	<Card name="Blade Dancer" /> works the same way.
	Her triggered ability reads "When you choose a friendly unit, you may exhaust me and pay <Universal /> to ready it."
	It ends up on the chain before the original spell can be countered.
</Callout>

## Does Blade Dancer's ability trigger Irelia, Fervent's +1 might? [#blade-dancer]

Not from choosing — but she can still trigger from being readied if she was exhausted.

<Card name="Blade Dancer" /> does not choose a unit — it does not target.<Rule number="355.10.d" /><Rule number="355.10.d.1" />
"It" in "ready it" deterministically refers back to the unit already identified as Blade Dancer's trigger condition, with no separate choice made by the player.
Irelia's ability can only trigger from Blade Dancer if she was exhausted and is readied by it — and only through the readying half of her ability, not the choosing half.<Rule number="415.1" />

## Does Irelia, Fervent get +1 might when she readies during the Awaken Phase? [#awaken-phase-readying]

Yes, but only if she was exhausted and actually transitions to the ready state.

During the Awaken Phase at the start of each turn, the turn player readies all game objects they control that are able to be readied.<Rule number="315.1.b" />
Readying is the action of marking a game object as available for action.<Rule number="415.1" />
If Irelia was exhausted, she transitions to ready and her trigger fires.

If she is already ready at the start of your turn, she cannot be readied again<Rule number="415.1.b" /> — no readying event occurs and the trigger does not fire.

## Does a Repeat spell targeting Irelia, Fervent for both instructions give her +1 might twice? [#repeat-targeting-twice]

Yes, as choosing her for each instruction is a separate triggering event.

<Repeat /> is functionally "execute the spell's instructions one additional time".<Rule number="820.1.d" />
When the Repeat cost is paid, choices for both executions are made during the same "Make relevant choices" step of playing the card.<Rule number="820.2" /><Rule number="820.2.a" />
Choosing Irelia for the first instruction and choosing her for the second target her twice.
After the spell finalizes, her targeting effect is added to the chain as a pending item once for each targeting event.<Rule number="383.4.b.2" /><Rule number="383.4.b.3" />

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
	You play <Card name="Feral Strength" /> and pay its <Repeat /> cost, choosing Irelia for both instructions.
	After Feral Strength finalizes, Irelia's ability triggers twice — once per targeting event — and both triggers are added to the chain.
	When her triggers resolve, she receives +1 and +1 from her own ability.
	When the spell resolves, Irelia receives +2 and +2 from the spell's two instructions.
	That's +6 might total this turn.
</Callout>
