## If Sacrifice is countered, is the killed unit returned? [#countered-additional-cost]

No.
Killing the friendly <Mighty /> unit is an additional cost paid before Sacrifice can be finalized.
Countering the spell does not return that unit because [paid costs are not refunded](/general-rules/costs-and-payments#countered-costs).

## Can my opponent interact before the chosen Mighty unit is killed? [#cost-reaction-window]

No.
The <Mighty /> unit is chosen and killed while Sacrifice is finalized, and [no normal reaction window opens while costs are being paid](/general-rules/costs-and-payments#cost-reaction-window).
The unit is not a target because it is referenced as part of a cost.<Rule number="355.10.c" />

Sacrifice's controller may activate an eligible <Add /> ability with <Reaction /> when resources are required.
This is a special permission for resource payment, not a normal reaction window, so it does not let an opponent play a spell such as <Card name="Stupefy" /> between choosing and killing the unit.
By the time a normal priority window opens, the cost has been paid and the unit is already in the trash.

## Does the Deathknell of the unit killed as the cost resolve before Sacrifice draws and channels? [#deathknell-before-spell]

Yes.
Killing the friendly <Mighty /> unit is a non-standard additional cost paid while Sacrifice is finalized.<Rule number="356.7" /><Rule number="357.2" />
A <Deathknell /> ability created [by killing a unit as a cost resolves before the card whose cost killed it](/mechanics/deathknell#killed-as-cost), so it resolves before Sacrifice draws and channels.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
  You kill <Card name="Rift Herald" />, whose <Deathknell /> reads "Play a unit from your hand to your base, ignoring its Energy cost," to pay Sacrifice's cost.
  The <Deathknell /> resolves first — you play a free unit from your pre-draw hand — then Sacrifice resolves and you draw 2 and channel 1 rune exhausted.
</Callout>
