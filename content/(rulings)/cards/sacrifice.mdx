## If Sacrifice is countered, is the killed unit returned? [#countered-additional-cost]

No, as the kill is an additional cost and countering a spell never refunds costs already paid.

Killing a friendly <Mighty /> unit is an additional cost required to play Sacrifice.
A countered spell does nothing and is cleared from the chain.<Rule number="425.1.a" />
Countering does not refund any costs paid to play a card, including additional costs.<Rule number="425.1.c" /><Rule number="425.1.c.1" />
That cost is paid before the spell resolves, so the killed unit is not returned.

## Can my opponent interact before the chosen Mighty unit is killed? [#cost-reaction-window]

No normal reaction window opens between choosing the <Mighty /> unit and killing it.

The unit killed as a cost is not a target — objects referenced as part of a cost are explicitly excluded from targeting.<Rule number="355.10.c" />

Costs are paid during step 4 of finalization, and finalization does not pass priority.<Rule number="357.2" /><Rule number="337.1.a" />
During that step, Sacrifice's controller may activate an eligible <Add /> ability with <Reaction /> when resources are required, even without priority.<Rule number="357.1.a" /><Rule number="429.3" /><Rule number="429.3.a" />
This limited exception does not create a normal reaction window or permit an opponent to play a spell like <Card name="Stupefy" /> between choosing and killing the unit.

By the time a normal priority window opens, the cost has been paid and the unit is already in the trash.

## Does the Deathknell of the unit killed as the cost resolve before Sacrifice draws and channels? [#deathknell-before-spell]

Yes.
The kill is a non-standard additional cost, not a resolution instruction, so it happens during finalization.
That puts the <Deathknell /> on top of Sacrifice on the chain, where it resolves first.

### Resolution Sequence [#deathknell-sequence]

1. Sacrifice's opening line — "as an additional cost to play this, kill a friendly <Mighty /> unit" — is a cost, not a resolution instruction.<Rule number="356.7" />
2. Costs are paid in step 4 of the play process, before Sacrifice has finished being finalized.<Rule number="357.2" />
3. When a unit with <Deathknell /> is killed to pay that cost, its <Deathknell /> ability is added to the chain as a pending item before the unit moves to the trash.<Rule number="428.1.a.1.b" /><Rule number="808.1.d.2" /> The chain now holds the <Deathknell /> above Sacrifice, which is still mid-finalization.
4. Sacrifice continues through step 5, the legality check, and step 6, becoming a finalized chain item.<Rule number="359.3.a" />
5. Because the <Deathknell /> is still pending, it completes its own finalization before play proceeds.<Rule number="359.3.b" />
6. Once both items are finalized, the chain resolves from the top down. The <Deathknell /> was added last, so it resolves first.
7. After the <Deathknell /> fully resolves, Sacrifice resolves, drawing 2 and channeling 1 rune exhausted.

<Callout type="idea" title="Example">
  You kill <Card name="Rift Herald" />, whose <Deathknell /> reads "Play a unit from your hand to your base, ignoring its Energy cost," to pay Sacrifice's cost.
  The <Deathknell /> resolves first — you play a free unit from your pre-draw hand — then Sacrifice resolves and you draw 2 and channel 1 rune exhausted.
</Callout>
