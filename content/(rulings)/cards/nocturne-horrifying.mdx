## Does Nocturne's ability apply when Burn or Minefield puts it into the trash? [#burn-and-minefield]

No.
Neither <Burn /> nor <Card name="Minefield" /> instructs you to look at or reveal Nocturne.

<Callout type="warn" title="Rules citation needed">
	"Look" is not yet defined as its own numbered action the way "reveal" is.
	The core rules consistently treat it as a parallel, explicitly-instructed action alongside reveal wherever both are mentioned, and this ruling relies on that pattern — an official update giving "look" its own definition would let this be cited directly.
</Callout>

Nocturne's ability reads "As you look at or reveal me from the top of your deck, you may banish me.
If you do, you may play me for <Universal />."
The word "as" identifies this as a replacement effect that can apply only while the qualifying look or reveal event is being processed.<Rule number="369.1" /><Rule number="370.1" />

Reveal is a defined action, and a player may only reveal cards when a game effect specifically instructs them to.<Rule number="424.1" /><Rule number="424.2" /><Rule number="424.2.a" />
"Look" does not have its own numbered definition yet; as the note above explains, treating it as an instructed-only action alongside reveal rests on intent rather than an explicit rule.

<Burn /> does neither of these — it puts cards from the top of your main deck directly into your trash, with no instruction to look at or reveal them.<Rule number="440.1" />
Minefield's printed ability, "When you conquer here, put the top 2 cards of your Main Deck into your trash," likewise contains no look or reveal instruction.
In either case, Nocturne becomes visible only after it reaches the trash, a public zone.<Rule number="108.2.d" />
